The Jehangir Art Gallery is one of Mumbai's most iconic art spaces and a landmark of the city's vibrant art scene. Established in 1952 by Sir Cowasji Jehangir, the gallery has played a pivotal role in promoting contemporary Indian art and providing a platform for artists from around the country. Nestled in the heart of South Mumbai near the Kala Ghoda precinct, it is a hub for artists, curators, and art enthusiasts alike. The gallery hosts a wide variety of exhibitions, ranging from modern art to traditional Indian art forms. It has become a cultural destination for both local and international visitors, offering a glimpse into the rich artistic heritage of India. The building itself is an architectural gem, with spacious interiors that provide the perfect setting for large-scale exhibitions and intimate artistic displays alike. Over the years, Jehangir Art Gallery has showcased works by some of India's most celebrated artists, including M.F. Husain, S.H. Raza, and Tyeb Mehta. The gallery's exhibitions cover a wide range of media, including painting, sculpture, photography, and installations.
