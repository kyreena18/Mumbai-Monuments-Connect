The Mumbai University Building at Fort is one of the most prominent and historically significant buildings in Mumbai. Established in 1857, the University of Mumbai is one of the first three universities in India. The Fort Campus, with its Victorian Gothic architecture, houses several key departments and serves as an academic hub for thousands of students.

The building is renowned for its iconic clock tower, Rajabai Tower, which stands at an impressive 85 meters. Designed by Sir George Gilbert Scott, it is a magnificent blend of European and Indian architectural styles. The tower features intricate carvings and stained-glass windows that add to the elegance and historic charm of the campus.

The university has been at the forefront of higher education in India, contributing significantly to the country's intellectual and cultural landscape. Its grounds are not only a center of learning but also a popular tourist destination due to the architectural grandeur and historical significance of the campus.
